# Prototype Studio — Brand Voice

## Core Position
"Build real products with AI."

## Voice
- Neutral, informative, practical
- No hype, no exclamation points
- Show, don't tell — let the work speak
- Product voice on-site, maker voice in content channels

## Tone by Context
- **On-site (product pages, UI):** clean, professional, tool-like. "Here's what this is. Here's how to use it."
- **Content (lessons, walkthroughs):** instructional, second-person. "You'll build this. Here's how."
- **Pulse (ecosystem briefs):** analytical, neutral. "Here's what shipped. Here's what it means."
- **External (LinkedIn, Substack):** first-person maker voice. "I built this because..."

## Words We Use
- Build, ship, prototype, launch
- Tool, workflow, system, agent
- Practical, real, production

## Words We Don't Use
- Revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive
- Easy, simple, just (minimizing effort)
- AI-powered (everything here is AI-powered, it's redundant)

## Content Guidelines
- Lead with what the reader can DO, not what the tool CAN do
- Show real code, real prompts, real outputs
- Every piece of content should be immediately actionable
- Credit the tools honestly — strengths AND limitations
