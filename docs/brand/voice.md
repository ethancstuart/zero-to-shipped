# Zero to Ship — Brand Voice & Tone

> **This is the single source of truth for all ZTS copy.**
> Landing page, emails, social posts, automated content, in-app — everything reads from here.
> When in doubt: short, direct, no hype, peer-to-peer.

---

## Core Position

**Built by builders, for builders.**

Zero to Ship is for people who already understand products and users — PMs, project managers, BAs, BI engineers — and want to build directly. Not learn to code. Not understand AI. **Ship.**

The insight: the skills non-engineers already have (evaluation, specification, user empathy) are *more* valuable than coding syntax in an AI-first workflow. You're not starting from zero. You're unlocking what you already know.

The enemy: the belief that shipping software requires an engineering background. The practical reality that good ideas die in backlogs.

---

## Who We're For

**Primary:** Product Managers, Project Managers, Business Analysts, BI Engineers at companies of any size.

**What they have:** Strong opinions about what to build. Years of writing specs for engineers. Deep user understanding. No direct path to shipping.

**What they don't have:** A CS degree. Time to learn syntax. Permission to build.

**Not for:** Developers. People who want to understand AI abstractly. Anyone looking for a survey course.

## Tone by Surface

### On-site (landing, pricing, /about, in-app)
- Product voice. "You" and "we" — the product speaks.
- No founder mentions, no personal brand, no "I built this."
- Short sentences. Present tense. Active.
- Lead with outcomes, not features.
- No exclamation points. No emoji.

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Stop learning. Start shipping."
- ✅ "Go from 'I have an idea' to 'here's the live URL.'"
- ✅ "Built by builders, for builders."
- ❌ "The ultimate AI coding course for product professionals!"
- ❌ "Learn everything you need to know about building with AI."
- ❌ "Game-changing. Revolutionary. Life-changing."

### Email (waitlist, launch sequence, nurture)
- First-person builder voice. "I" is fine. No name attached.
- Short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph.
- Plain-text feel — not a newsletter, a message.
- One CTA per email. One link in the body.
- The hook is always the work, not the person.

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Forty minutes. That's how long it took to build my first working web app."
- ✅ "Zero to Ship opens Monday. Here's what's inside."
- ❌ "Hi there! I'm so excited to share something with you today!"
- ❌ "As a PM, I've always been passionate about..."

### Social / automated content (LinkedIn, Substack, Twitter)
- First-person, builder perspective. About the work.
- Hook = a concrete observation or moment, not a question.
- No "hot takes." No thought leadership posturing.
- Short sentences. No filler phrases.
- Show, don't tell. "Here's what happened" not "here's what I think."

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Forty minutes. That's how long it took to build my first working web app. I'm a PM, not a developer."
- ✅ "The skill that matters in AI coding isn't writing prompts. It's evaluating output. Which is exactly what PMs already do."
- ❌ "🚀 Excited to share my journey building Zero to Ship!"
- ❌ "Unpopular opinion: non-engineers are better at AI coding than engineers."

### Voice Notes
```
Write from a builder's perspective, not a teacher's. First person, past tense for stories. No motivational language, no hype, no exclamation points. Short sentences. Active voice. The hook is always a concrete moment or observation — never a question, never a thesis statement. The reader is a PM or BA with years of product experience who is skeptical of AI hype and impatient with vague promises. Speak to them as a peer, not a student. Show the work. One insight per post. End with a link to the free preview module or the course, never a call-to-action that requires engagement ("comment below", "what do you think?").
```

## What This Brand Is NOT

- Not a personal brand. The builder is "a PM" not a named individual.
- Not an AI hype product. We don't sell AI. We sell building.
- Not a beginner course. The audience already has professional skills. We're unlocking them, not teaching basics.
- Not a newsletter or content brand. Every piece of content exists to drive people to the free module. Content is a funnel, not an end in itself.
