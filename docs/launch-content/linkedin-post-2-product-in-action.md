## Draft

Forty minutes.

That's how long it took me to build my first working web app with an AI coding tool. I'm a Product Manager, not a developer. I've never shipped a line of production code in my life.

The app was a habit tracker. Nothing revolutionary. Local storage, a streak counter, a simple UI. But it ran in my browser, I could send the URL to my wife, and she could actually use it.

I sent the link. They added a habit. They watched the streak counter update.

That moment reframed everything I thought about AI coding.

For the last six months I've been building Zero to Ship — a course that teaches PMs, BAs, project managers, and BI engineers how to build real software with tools like Cursor and Claude Code. I'm launching it on April 28.

Here's the thing I keep coming back to: the skill that matters isn't writing code. It isn't even writing prompts. It's evaluating output — looking at what an AI just gave you and knowing what's wrong with it.

Which is, not coincidentally, the skill non-engineers already have.

If you've ever written a PRD, drawn a wireframe, or modeled a process, you have the foundation for this. What you don't have is permission. I'm going to give it to you.
